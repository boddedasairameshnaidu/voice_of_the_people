Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I submit a complaint?
A: To submit a complaint, go to the File a complaint and click on the "Submit Complaint" button. Fill in the required information and submit the form.
Q: How long does it take to resolve a complaint?
A: The time taken to resolve a complaint can vary depending on the nature and complexity of the issue. Our team strives to address complaints as quickly as possible, and you will be updated on the progress throughout the resolution process.
Q: What types of complaints can I submit?
A: You can submit complaints related to various issues such as public safety, environmental concerns, municipal services, noise complaints, and more. The portal provides specific categories to choose from when submitting your complaint.
Q: Is there a fee for submitting a complaint?
A: No, submitting a complaint through the citizen complaint portal is free of charge. It is a service provided by the government to facilitate efficient complaint resolution and community engagement.
Q: Can I provide additional information or evidence for my complaint?
A: Yes, you can provide additional information or supporting evidence for your complaint. After submitting the initial complaint, you may receive instructions on how to upload or attach any relevant documents, photos, or videos that can help in the investigation and resolution process.
Q: What if I'm not satisfied with the resolution of my complaint?
A: If you are not satisfied with the resolution of your complaint, you may have options to escalate the issue. The portal provides a mechanism to request a review or appeal, depending on the nature of the complaint. Additional information on the escalation process will be provided along with the complaint resolution.
